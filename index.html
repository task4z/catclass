Our Story

A Legacy Built on Excellence

Founded in 1952, Riverside High School has spent over seven decades shaping the minds and characters of young people in Millbrook and the greater California community. What began as a modest campus of 300 students has grown into one of the state's most celebrated public high schools — without ever losing sight of what matters most: the individual student.

We believe that a great education is not the delivery of information alone, but the cultivation of curiosity, resilience, and empathy. Every program, every extracurricular, every hallway conversation is designed with that belief at its core.

Our alumni lead Fortune 500 companies, win Olympic medals, create art that moves nations, and serve their communities with quiet dignity. We are proud of every one of them — and of the students who will join their ranks.

Dr. Margaret Holloway
Principal, Riverside High School
Academic Excellence

Six Pillars of Learning

From Advanced Placement to the arts, our programs are designed to challenge every student and unlock their unique potential.

01 — Humanities

Classical Humanities

Students engage with the foundational texts of civilization, developing rhetoric, critical thinking, and the empathy required to lead in a complex world.

02 — STEM

Science Engineering

State-of-the-art labs and a direct university partnership allow students to conduct college-level research from their sophomore year onward.

03 — Arts

Visual Performing Arts

Conservatory-style programs in music, theater, dance, and fine arts develop disciplined mastery and authentic creative expression.

04 — Technology

Computer Science AI

Our coding pathways — from web development to machine learning — prepare students for the digital economy of tomorrow.

05 — Global

World Languages IB

Six world languages, Model UN, and an International Baccalaureate Diploma pathway for globally-minded, culturally fluent students.

06 — Leadership

Civic Student Leadership

ASB, National Honor Society, and service-learning academies build the next generation of community leaders — starting in ninth grade.
